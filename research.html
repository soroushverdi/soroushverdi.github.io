Overview of research

My research lies at the intersection of consumer behavior, digital marketing, and empirical methods. I am particularly interested in how consumers respond to algorithmic recommendations, personalized pricing, and platform design choices in online marketplaces.

A central theme of my work is understanding how firms can design marketing interventions that are both effective and ethically responsible. I investigate how transparency, fairness, and perceived control influence consumer trust and long-term engagement.

Methodologically, I rely on a combination of:

  • Randomized field experiments with industry partners.
  • Large-scale observational datasets from digital platforms.
  • Structural and reduced-form econometric models.

My projects cover several focus areas:

  • Algorithmic recommendations: how recommender systems influence consumer search, consideration sets, and satisfaction.
  • Pricing and promotions: consumer responses to dynamic pricing, personalized offers, and coupons in online retail.
  • Consumer privacy and trust: how data collection and disclosure policies shape attitudes toward firms and platforms.

Across these areas, my goal is to generate insights that are theoretically grounded, empirically rigorous, and directly relevant for practitioners designing marketing strategies in digital markets.
