Overview of research

I study the psychology of belief, metacognition, reasoning, and social judgment — particularly how people evaluate their own and others’ cognitive states.

In one line of work, I study the lay ethics of belief: how people think beliefs should form, what they consider rational, and how they judge biased thinking.

I also study implicit theories of reasoning, including how people attribute responsibility for reasoning errors and when they think beliefs can change.

  • How people judge the rationality of beliefs
  • How they assess responsibility for biased thinking
  • How they attribute freedom or control over mental states
