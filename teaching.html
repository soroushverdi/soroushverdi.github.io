Teaching philosophy

My teaching emphasizes developing students’ ability to think critically about marketing decisions in data-rich environments. I aim to create an inclusive classroom where students feel comfortable asking questions, challenging assumptions, and engaging with real-world marketing problems.

I integrate case studies, hands-on data exercises, and collaborative projects to help students connect conceptual frameworks with managerial practice. Clear expectations, timely feedback, and open communication are central to my approach.
